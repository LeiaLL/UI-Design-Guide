Typography

Guidelines and Good Practices

  1. Pair 2-3 well paired fonts to create contrast between text elements
  2. Web safe fonts work across all browsers, you can add additional fonts but note that those fonts may not be supported by the users browser, therefore add backup fonts
  3. Ensure appropriate spacing between words, letters, lines and text blocks
  4. Ensure good colour contrast between text and their background
  5. Ensure appropriate sizing of paragraph elements
  6. Use <H1> for titles , <H2> for headings & <H3> for subheadings

Colour

Guidelines and Good Practices

  1. Choose a colour scheme based on colour theory
  2. Use colour combinations that enhance your content. Colors reflect emotions - keep this in mind when choosing colours
  3. Brand colours should dominate the color palette to build user recogniction
  4. Choose an accent colour to draw attention to specific elements that require the users attention
  5. Never use blue as a general accent color
  6. Consider accessibilty when choosing your colour palette
  7. Whitespace is important
  8. Use neon colors very sparingly
  9. Design is important, but usability is key!

Structure

Guidelines and Good Practices

  1. People remember the first and the last element of the website above anything else (Primary and Recency Effect)
  2. F-shape skimming - readers scan text from the left to the right and from the top to the buttom
  3. Avoid long blocks of text, 50-75 characters per line (Miller’s Law)
  4. Place related elements in common areas (Law of Common Region) and grouped elements close to each other (Law of Proximity)
  5. Keep users choices to a minimum (Hick’s Law & Choice Overload Bias)
  6. Use simple structures and avoid complex shapes (Law of Prägnanz)
  7. Follow conventions and standards to make it easy to follow the page layout

Navigation

Guidelines and Good Practices

  1. the structure is important for navigation
  2. Have a primary navigation and a secondary navigation system
  3. Use size, weight, font and colour to highlight important text elements to draw the users attention
  4. Use signifiers to indicate how to interact with objects and elements on the page & visualise what elements are interactive
  5. Having text on buttons is important to remove uncertainty
  6. Use skeudomorphissm to help unexperienced/untrained users interact with the website
  7. Make the main actionable targets easy to reach (Fitts’ Law)
  8. Use familiar scenarios and logic when designing the websites behaviour (Jakob’s Law)
